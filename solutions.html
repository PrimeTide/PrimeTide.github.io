What Talos handles

The specific work that moves quietly in the background

Talos focuses on work that is repeatable, predictable, and easier for a system to manage than a tired agent at the end of a long day. You stay in charge of judgment calls and real conversations.

Lead intake
New names becoming real, organized records.
  • Website and portal inquiries pushed into your CRM with basic source and tags.
  • Simple checks to prevent obvious duplicates from cluttering your database.
  • Clear fields for how they found you and what they’re hoping to do.
First response
Fast, calm replies that sound like you.
  • Short, human messages that acknowledge the inquiry and set an expectation for next steps.
  • Simple branching: immediate response vs. “after hours” pacing when you prefer slower evenings.
  • Automatic pause whenever you’ve already replied yourself.
Light follow-up
Staying present without hovering.
  • Gentle check-ins for “not yet” buyers and sellers over days, weeks, or months.
  • Daily lists in your CRM that highlight who actually needs attention today.
  • Automatic removal from sequences when a real conversation opens up.
Back-end hygiene
Less clutter, clearer next steps.
  • Basic cleanup of tags, stages, and duplicate records.
  • Routing rules so certain sources or price ranges land where they belong.
  • Simple calendar confirmations for consult calls and key milestones.
How it feels in practice

Where the system steps in, and where it steps back

The goal is not to automate your whole business. The goal is to remove the pieces that pull you out of real work with people.

Work Talos is well suited for

Simple, repeatable tasks that follow a clear pattern and don’t require nuance from call to call.

  • Catching and logging every new inquiry, even during showings.
  • Sending a short “I saw this” message when leads first reach out.
  • Light check-ins for long-term prospects with no urgency.
  • Cleaning up obvious data issues in the background.
Work that stays in your hands

Conversations where tone, judgment, and local expertise matter more than speed.

  • Advising on offers, pricing, and negotiation strategy.
  • Any message where someone shares something personal or sensitive.
  • Nuanced back-and-forth with active buyers and sellers.
  • Decisions about which relationships to lean into next.
Process

From scattered tools to a steady first build

A “solution” here is not a pre-built template. It’s a small, intentional set of automations tuned to your current tools and your way of working.

1
Map your current week
One short call, a few screenshots, no homework.

We walk through how leads arrive today, where you spend manual effort, and where opportunities are lost or delayed. The outcome is a simple sketch of your tools and the biggest leaks.

2
Design a small, specific change
Usually focused on intake and first response.

Together we choose one narrow slice to improve first. You approve the language and timing before anything goes live, and we keep the rest of your systems untouched.

3
Run, observe, refine
Adjustments over weeks, not one big launch.

Once real leads run through the new flow, we tune pacing, adjust messages, and decide together whether to extend Talos into follow-up or operations next.
