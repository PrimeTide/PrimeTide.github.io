Process

From scattered tools to a steady system

The shape is simple: understand your current week, fix the biggest leaks, then refine once real leads run through.

1
Map what already exists
One short call and a few screenshots.

We walk through how leads arrive today and where they fall off. The outcome is a clear picture of your tools and a small list of places Talos can help first.

2
Build the first slice
Usually intake, first reply, and basic tagging.

Talos is wired between your tools to fix those leaks. You approve the language and timing before anything goes live.

3
Tune and extend
Adjustments over weeks, not one big launch.

As leads move through, we adjust pacing, trim steps that feel heavy, and add new automations only where they clearly help.

Example flows

What this looks like in real days

These aren’t scripts to copy — just snapshots of how Talos usually shows up in an agent’s week.

New portal inquiry
From “just came in” to organized lead
  • Lead arrives from a portal and lands in your CRM with source and price range tagged.
  • A short, human reply goes out in your voice with one or two simple questions.
  • If they reply, the automation steps back. If they don’t, Talos schedules a gentle follow-up.
Open house signup
From sign-in sheet to clear next steps
  • Attendees are added to your CRM the same day with basic notes attached.
  • Everyone receives a brief thank-you and a useful link or recap.
  • Hot leads are flagged for a call; others move into a light nurture path.
Long-term nurture
Staying present without hovering
  • “Someday” buyers are grouped by timeline instead of one big bucket.
  • Every so often, they receive a small, relevant check-in that still sounds like you.
  • Any reply moves them into a more active follow-up flow; silence does not trigger a barrage.
